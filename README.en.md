# NeuroFarmac: Neuropharmacology platform on a chip

[![DOI](https://zenodo.org/badge/DOI/10.5281/zenodo.19393993.svg)](https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19393993)
[![License](https://img.shields.io/badge/License-Proprietary-red.svg)](LICENSE)
[![ES](https://img.shields.io/badge/Spanish-version-green.svg)](./README.md)

**An in vitro system combining brain organoids, functional blood-brain barrier, and real-time electrical monitoring to evaluate drugs without using animals.**

## What problem does it solve?

Drugs for brain diseases (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, epilepsy, etc.) fail 90% of the time because current models do not predict what happens in the human brain:

- 2D cells do not resemble real brain tissue.
- Animals are not human: what works in mice fails in people.
- It is not measured whether the drug crosses the blood-brain barrier (BBB).
- Traditional tests measure cell death, not neuronal activity.

**NeuroFarmac solves all of this in one system.**

## What does it do?

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **3D human brain organoid** | Miniature brain tissue (2–4 mm) with neurons and glia |
| **Functional blood-brain barrier (BBB)** | Simulates the filter that drugs must cross |
| **High-density microelectrode array (MEA)** | Monitors neuronal electrical activity in real time |

The drug is injected into the flow, crosses the BBB (or not), reaches the organoid, and the electrodes measure whether neuronal activity changes.

## What can be evaluated?

- **BBB permeability**: does the drug cross the barrier?
- **Effect on neuronal activity**: does it increase, decrease, or desynchronize?
- **Functional toxicity**: does the neuronal network collapse? Does it recover?

## Who is it for?

- Neuroscience and pharmacology laboratories.
- Pharmaceutical companies developing brain drugs.
- Research centers in neurological diseases.
- Regulators needing more reliable data than animal models.
